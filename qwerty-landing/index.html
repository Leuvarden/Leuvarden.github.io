Pigeon post is a simple casual game with a pinch of puzzles and history. It tells the story about war pigeons during World War I.
During the First and Second World Wars, carrier pigeons were largely used to transport messages. For example, the US Army Signal Corps used 600 pigeons in France alone.
A carrier pigeon's job was dangerous. Nearby enemy soldiers often tried to shoot down pigeons, knowing that released birds were carrying important messages. Some of these pigeons became quite famous among the infantrymen they worked for. In our game we tell the story about blue check hen named Cher Ami.
Cher Ami is known for saving about 200 men of the Lost Battalion of the 77th Division in the Battle of the Argonne, October 1918.
